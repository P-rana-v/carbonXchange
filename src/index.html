What is Carbon Emmision Exchange

As the global climate change issues are increasingly concerned by the international community, all countries are actively seeking ways of reducing emissions of greenhouse gases. Emissions trading is a market-based approach to controlling pollution by providing economic incentives for reducing the emissions of pollutants such as carbon dioxide (CO2). In a carbon emission exchange, the government can allocate or sell carbon emission credits regularly, and enterprises can also trade their carbon emissions in this market. At present, the world's largest greenhouse gas emission trading market is The European Union Emission Trading System (or EU-ETS). It operates in all EU countries plus Iceland, Liechtenstein and Norway. Some other countries, such as UK, China, are also actively building their own carbon emission markets. However, in these markets, most of them adopt the single-side auctions such as pricing transaction and bidding methods which are considered not economic-efficient. We intend to build a decentralized double auction platform for carbon emission exchange to tackle this problem. So that carbon emissions trading can be processed more efficiently and safely.
